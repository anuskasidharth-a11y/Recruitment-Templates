# Offer Acceptance Follow-up

## Purpose

This template is used to follow up with candidates after the offer has been released but before they have shared their decision. The objective is to understand whether they have any questions, address concerns proactively, and maintain a positive candidate experience without creating unnecessary pressure.

---

## Subject

Follow-up on Your Offer | **[Company Name]**

---

## Email Template

Hi **[Candidate Name]**,

I wanted to check in regarding the offer we recently shared for the **[Job Title]** position at **[Company Name]**.

I understand that accepting a new opportunity is an important career decision, and you may be taking time to evaluate all aspects of the offer.

I wanted to see if you had any questions regarding the role, compensation, benefits, team structure, joining process, or any other aspect of the opportunity. I'd be happy to clarify anything that would help you make an informed decision.

If it works for you, we can also connect over a quick 10 to 15 minute call at a time that's convenient for you.

Looking forward to hearing from you, and I hope to welcome you to the team soon.

Best regards,

**[Recruiter Name]**

Talent Acquisition

**[Company Name]**

---

## When to Use

- One to three business days after the offer has been released.
- When the candidate has not yet responded.
- When the recruiter wants to proactively resolve concerns before the offer deadline.

---

## Best Practices

- Give candidates adequate time before following up.
- Focus on understanding concerns rather than persuading the candidate.
- Be supportive and professional throughout the conversation.
- Offer a call if the candidate would prefer to discuss the offer.
- End with a clear invitation for questions.
